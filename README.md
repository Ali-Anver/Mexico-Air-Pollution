Dust in the errors: Comparative econometric measurements on Mexico’s Hoy no Circula

Abstract:

Regression Discontinuity Designs (RDD) often have measurement error in their running
variables, which can lead to invalid inferences if not properly addressed. In this study,
we replicate and extend Davis’s (2008) analysis of car restriction policies and air qual-
ity in Mexico City by integrating Dong and Kolesar (2023) methodological recommenda-
tions —bias-aware confidence intervals and donut trimming - alongside our own choice
of optimal bandwidth selection to address measurement error. Additionally, we employ
semi-parametric estimation as an external validation of our approach. Under very con-
servative bias adjustments, our results remain inconclusive; however, when more realistic
assumptions are imposed, we document a statistically significant reduction in air pollutants
in the short run (weeks after the policy was implemented), with subsequent evidence of
a rebound effect afterward. Our enhanced framework not only refines causal inference in
contexts with imprecisely measured running variables but also offers both theoretical and
empirical advancements for RDD applications in similar scenarios.
